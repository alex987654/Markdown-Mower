Markdown Mower

A tokenizer-agnostic linter for Markdown destined for an LLM. Reduces token cost by removing what AI models usually do not need and what human readers may not necessarily consider essential.

v0.1 runs in your browser

Marginalia

Toggle individual rules. Three rules are off by default — they change document rendering and require Aggressive mode.

Why a tokenizer-agnostic linter?

Tokenizer counts change with every model release and don't agree across vendors. Rather than pretend one number is "the" token cost, this tool gives a range built from two well-known rules of thumb (≈ chars/4.5 to chars/3.2, with corrections for newlines, repeated-character runs, and non-ASCII) and focuses on universal patterns that cost more in every tokenizer: padded tables, decorative banners, whole-paragraph emphasis, repeated URLs, hedge phrases, invisible whitespace.

Inspired in part by SkillReducer (arXiv:2603.29919) and the broader "less-is-more" finding that removing non-essential content tends to improve agent quality, not just reduce cost. This linter handles the part of that work that doesn't require a language model.

Source on GitHub. Open in any browser; no server, no telemetry, no data leaves the page.
